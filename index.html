How Startup Financing Works

by Andrew Boardman

Founders own 100% of a company until they raise money. You then have two levers to determine ownership after the investment.

Pre-Money Valuation
and
Investment Size

Stock options are created from the Option Pool, shares set aside by the company to attract and retain key employees.

The size of the
Option Pool
is often specified as a percentage of total shares.
It can be created from either the
Pre-Money
Post-Money
side.
A popular way to do early financing is through Convertible Debt, which allows the company to raise money while deferring the Valuation of the company until the next Financing Round, which must take place before the Term is up (or the debt must be renegotiated) to avoid having to pay it back to the investor, often with a Dividend.
To compensate the (usually) Angel Investors for taking the risk of lending their money to the company at an unknown Valuation, they normally ask for a
Discount
to the next round's Share Price, and a Pre-Money
Valuation Cap
, which does not affect other investors for that Financing Round. The
Convertible Debt
converts into the same type of stock as the next round of financing, with the same terms.
